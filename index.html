Science fiction writers have long imagined a future of computer interaction mediated by intelligent, sentient interfaces like HAL 9000, and their depictions have ranged from sensitive companion to outright menace. With advances in machine learning and natural language processing, many of their functions have crept into our everyday lives in common internet applications like Google AdSense and Netflix's movie suggestions. But the sentient interface itself has lagged behind, and has had a decidedly checkered history. Early interations like Clippy the Microsoft Office assistant were poorly designed and were likewise poorly received. Siri was released to much fanfare, but high expectations inevitably outstripped her modest capabilities and spotty execution. Siri has arguably gained the most traction as branding for Apple, or failing that, as a source of amusement.

A better approach to interactive artifical intelligence may be to design it explicitly for entertainment: more Virtual Pet than Virtual Assistant. Tamagotchi became a wild success in the 90's, despite a limited set of interactions. Today a desktop or mobile phone companion could use natural language processing and the entire internet as a resource, gamifying the collection of user data and returning links to curated content. I call my idea... wait for it... Turing Petz! It's almost a pun on Turing Test but not quite.

Once the framework has been built, the possibilities for the Turing Pet for mediating information tasks are limitless. The Turing Pet could handle reminders and to-do lists, recommend products. It could serve as a platform for productivity management tools like the Pomodoro method, or for language tutoring. Most importantly, it'd be a natural vehicle for advertising; without the bandwidth demands of regular websites, relevant ads could be delivered in a quantity that prioritizes the user experience. Your donation would fund a text-based prototype and you'd get a chance to participate in the development process.
